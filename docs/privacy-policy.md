# Privacy Policy — SmartDeal Hunter

**Last updated**: May 7, 2026

SmartDeal Hunter is a privacy-first Amazon shopping assistant. We believe that
your shopping habits, preferences, and data belong to you. This policy
describes how we handle your information.

## 1. Zero Data Collection

By default, SmartDeal Hunter **does not collect, transmit, or store any of your
data on remote servers**.

- **No Tracking**: We do not use cookies, tracking pixels, or analytics SDKs.
- **No Telemetry**: We do not collect "usage data" or "diagnostic data."
- **No Advertising**: We do not sell your data or use it for advertising.

## 2. On-Device Storage and Encryption

All data generated by the extension is stored **locally on your device** within
your browser's isolated storage (IndexedDB and `chrome.storage.local`).

- **Genome Profile**: Your preference vector (the "Genome") is encrypted at
  rest using **AES-GCM-256**. The encryption key is derived from your
  unique local session and is never persisted to disk. We use PBKDF2 with
  600,000 iterations for key derivation.
- **Price History**: Prices you explicitly scout are stored locally to
  provide price-history charts. These are never shared.
- **Product Cache**: To improve performance, we keep a small local cache of
  analyzed products.

## 3. Optional External Access (Deep Check)

The "Deep Check" feature is an **optional, explicit opt-in** service that
retrieves live product data from the Amazon Creators API.

- **Explicit Consent**: This feature is disabled by default. It only activates
  if you explicitly click "Connect to Amazon" and complete the OAuth flow.
- **Encrypted Tokens**: Your Amazon OAuth tokens are stored locally and
  encrypted using the same AES-GCM-256 mechanism described above.
- **Revocation**: You can revoke access at any time from the extension's
  Privacy tab or your Amazon account settings.

### Review authenticity (added in v0.2.1)

When you Quick Scout a product page, the extension reads the review block
already loaded in your browser tab and computes an authenticity score
locally using six heuristics. The review text is processed in memory and
discarded as soon as the score is rendered — it is never persisted to
storage, never written to the audit log, and never transmitted off the
device. If you have opted in to the audit log, only the product ASIN,
the number of reviews scored, and the integer score are recorded.

## 4. Gemini Nano and On-Device ML

Scoring and sentiment analysis are performed **entirely on your device**.

- **Local Inference**: We use Google's Gemini Nano (if available in your
  browser) or a local keyword heuristic to analyze review text.
- **No Cloud ML**: We do not send your text to cloud-based LLMs for
  processing.

## 5. Data Control

You have full control over your data:

- **View Data**: You can view your raw Genome data in the Options page.
- **Export**: You can export your data to a JSON file.
- **Wipe**: You can wipe all local data instantly or schedule a recurring
  automatic wipe from the Privacy tab.
- **Uninstall**: If you uninstall the extension, all locally stored data
  managed by the extension is deleted by the browser.

## 6. Permissions

We only ask for the permissions necessary to function:

- `activeTab`: To read the product page you are currently viewing.
- `scripting`: To show the score panel on the Amazon page.
- `storage`: To save your settings and Genome locally.
- `alarms`: To handle scheduled data wipes and (since v0.2.0) the periodic
  on-device price-alert re-check (~30 min interval, evaluates only
  locally collected price history; never scrapes or makes remote calls).
- `offscreen`: To run on-device ML inference.
- `notifications` (since v0.2.0): To show OS-level price-drop alerts when
  you have explicitly enrolled an ASIN via the "Watch this ASIN" toggle.
  Clicking a notification focuses an already-open tab matching the
  cached product URL; we never open new tabs. Notifications can be
  disabled by un-enrolling each ASIN, by denying notification permission
  at the browser/OS level, or by wiping all data.
